About Us

Jurong Bird Park is a world-famous bird zoo where there are specimens of magnificent bird life from around the world, including a large flock of flamingos. It is currently the world's largest bird park in terms of the number of birds, and second largest both in the number of bird species and land area (after Germany's Weltvogelpark Walsrode). There are 5,000 birds of 400 species in Jurong Bird Park, of which 29 are of threatened species. In 2006, Jurong Bird Park completed a S$10 million makeover. As a result of the upgrade, the park got a new entrance plaza, a park-owned and managed Bongo Burgers restaurant, an ice cream parlour, a gift shop and a bird hospital. On 1 June 2016, we announced that Jurong Bird Park would be relocated to Mandai Lake Road in 2010. However, due to the impact of the Covid-19 pandemic, the move is pushed back to 2022. Kings of the Skies

An exciting show featuring the awesome abilities of the birds of prey such as the white-tailed sea eagle, hooded vulture, Harris's hawk, and Malay Fish Owl. Showcasing the fierce and the formidable, the Kings of the Skies Show stars birds of prey ‚Äì majestic creatures at the top of their food chain.


Witness the presence of magnificent predators such as the white-tailed sea eagle, the hooded vulture and the Harris's hawk. Also, discover how the Malay fish owl catch prey with its strong, steady talons. Look out for the scavenging vultures too. Brace yourself as these ravenous birds circle the grounds. And keep your eagle eyes focused on the sky as the brahminy kite flies in for a visit. You may also get up close and personal with these amazing predators!

Wetlands

With their combination of water, nutrients and warmth, tropical wetlands are bustling concentrations of life in many forms and magnets for birds. Stop by the Wetlands exhibit and spend some quiet time bird-watching from the viewing gallery, designed to simulate an observation hide.

Hornbills and Toucans

27 spacious and naturally landscaped aviaries over an area of 2,000m¬≤ house our collection of these beautiful birds. Guests are often greeted upon entry by a cacophony of loud honks. We have 19 hornbill species and Jurong Bird Park is the first to breed the black hornbill under human care.

African Treetops

An estimated 2,500 avian species from 111 families have been recorded in Africa and its neighbouring islands like Madagascar and Seychelles. Simulating a 360-degree walk through a rainforest canopy, the top tier of the feeding tower leads you right into the middle of the aviary. On cooler days, the birds can be seen flying together in iridescent flocks. Journey down to the lower tier and you may see ground-dwelling birds forage for food near the forest ground.

Birds of prey are also known as raptors, a term which is derived from the Latin word raptare, meaning ‚Äúseize and carry away‚Äù. These birds are characterised by their sharp talons, hooked beaks and large penetrating eyes. Guests at the Bird Park can catch a glimspse of their majestic aura at the Bird of Prey exhibit, or expiernece first-hand their amazing aerial prowess at the Kings of the Skies show, hosted at the Hawk Arena! Lory Loft

One of our four walk-in flight aviaries, Lory Loft is home to over ten species of gloriously-coloured lories and lorikeets. Visitors can hand-feed the lories with special mixture of imported lory dry mix, honey, and water! The expiernece of having a bold, screeching bundle of colours drink greedily from your hand is so unforgettable that is been chosen as a Top 10 Best Family Experience" by the Singapore Tourism Board!

Did You Know? Lories are also known as 'brush-tongued parrots', in reference to the elongated papillae (hair-life projections) on the tip of their tongues!

Wings of Asia

With over 500 birds from 135 species, this exhibit houses the largest diversity of birds in the park. 24 of these species are threatened in the wild. These include the rare Santa Cruz ground-dove, Bali myna and black-winged myna, all of which have been successfully bred at the park.

Waterfall Aviary

The Waterfall Aviary towers at a height of 35m (equivalent to 13 storeys!) and houses one of the world's tallest man-made, indoor waterfalls. Cross the suspended bridge for a panoramic view of the surrounding landscape and hike up to the Hut to catch sight of the some of the more reticent birds in action. One will feel as though he is living with the birds as these birds roam around freely! The Waterfall Aviary is a definite MUST NOT MISS !

Pelican Cove

Besides the Dalmatian and the brown, species like the American white, African pink-backed, Asian spot-billed and Australian complete our cosmopolitan collection. Catch the inhabitants of the Cove expertly manipulating their huge beaks at the world‚Äôs first underwater pelican viewing gallery.

Flamingo Lake

With their gorgeous plumage and leggy beauty, the flamingos have long enthralled avid shutterbugs at the Bird Park. We have bred flamingos successfully since 1972, a year after the official opening of the Park. Today, a few hundred pink-hued greater flamingos strut their style at the Lake. One can find the Greater Flamingo here!

Flamingo Pool

After the success of the natural open concept of the Flamingo Lake, which houses the greater flamingos, the Flamingo Pool was created in 1984 and became an instant hit with guests as well. One can find the Carribean Flamingo and Lesser Flamingo here!

Breeding and Research Centre (BRC)

Our bird nannies work round the clock to provide ‚Äòeggs-pert‚Äô care for the eggs and chicks in their charge. Among many others, the chicks of the critically endangered Bali myna and blue-throated macaw have prospered under their tender loving watch. Join us on the journey of an egg here!


Incubation Room

Abandoned eggs and eggs of rare and endangered birds are brought here to improve their chances of hatching. Eggs are candled to check for fertilisation before they are incubated. Automatic egg turners in the incubators do what parent bird do - warm the eggs evenly and prevent embryonic membrane from sticking to the shells. The temperature and humidity of the incubators are controlled and the lights are dimmed to simulate natural nest conditions. Talk about technology!


Nursery Room

Hatchlings are brought here and housed in brooders, providing a sound-proof and controlled environment to keep them safe and warm. Our bird nannies have a hectic schedule as the chicks have to be fed every two or three hours! Their food must be served at a temperature ranging from 40 - 44¬∞C. Anything hotter will hurt the chick's crop, the expandable pouch used by birds to store food. Food served cold cannot be digested by the chick!

Penguin Coast

Think cold ice and wind in the Antarctica. Then imagine waves gently lapping on an African coastline. Penguins are not only found in cold places, they have also made their homes on hot terrain! To find out more, march on down to Penguin Coast!


Indoor Exhibit

The 1,630m¬≤ indoor exhibit features the King Penguin, Humboldt, and Rockhopper species! Temperatures are kept at 10 - 15¬∞C and a special lighting system is used to recreate the four seasons which helps our penguins maintain their bio-rhythm. Isn't that cool? (pun intended)


Ourdoor Exhibit

At the outdoor exhibit, a commotion ensures whenever a butterfly of dragon flits in. At the sight of one, the entire colony of African penguins gives chase, tumbling into the water in their excitement! How cute is that!

In calmer moments, breeding pairs are seen guarding their nests at the air-conditioned den. See if you can glimplse a fluffy chick or two!

Our penguins are trained to head back to their den before the park closes. Stay on to view our evening penguin 'parade'.
